About Us

Our School's Background information

Lanet Secondary is a Mixed day School. It was established in 1982 on a seven acre land donated by the Kenya Defence Forces and added 6 more acres in 2020. The Municipal Council of Nakuru constructed the first four classrooms. The school together with MOE and sponsor have continued to expand the infrastructure of the school up to date; enabling the school to have facilities of six streams in Form one and two, Five streams in form Three and four streams in form Four. The school has three Science laboratories, one Home science laboratory, one Computer laboratory, One Library.

Lanet Secondary school is currently a Sub County mixed day school. The school population has been growing steadily over the years having started as a single stream school to the current status with a student population of 1131 learners. In academic performance the school is one of the best public mixed day schools in Nakuru East Sub Country. In 2020 KCSE Exam, 12 of the candidates were admitted to the university. The school infrastructure has continued to improve. Modern facilities have been put in place to cater for the growing population. Currently the school has 15 Classrooms with a shortfall of 7 classrooms. There are 3 Science laboratories each having a capacity of 60 students as well as a Home Science lab with a capacity of 45 students. There is need for a bigger and more appropriate Home Science room. There is a Computer laboratory with a Capacity of 30 Students making it difficult for the many students who want to take computer studies. To solve this problem, there is need for a bigger computer laboratory with a seating capacity of seventy (70). The school has 1 library, 1 Geography room, 1 French room, 1 Kitchen (Semi permanent). There are 3 water sources – Rain water, Kenya Military Academy piped water (rationed) and water bowsers. A borehole is urgently needed in the school. There are 11 boys’ toilets with one Special need toilet, which serves the boys at a ratio 30:1 with a shortfall of 7 boys’ toilets. Girls Toilets are 13, at a ratio of 25:1.
